OSHA General Industry Safety Excerpts (Summary)

Document ID: osha-general-001

Hot Work (29 CFR 1910.252)
Employers must implement controls to prevent fire and explosion during welding and cutting operations. Combustible materials must be relocated or protected. Fire watches are required where other than a minor fire might develop.

Confined Spaces (29 CFR 1910.146)
Permit-required confined spaces must have a written program including atmospheric testing, attendant monitoring, and rescue procedures. Oxygen-deficient atmospheres (below 19.5%) are immediately dangerous to life and health.

Hazard Communication
Employers must ensure workers are informed of chemical hazards in their work areas and trained on protective measures before exposure.

Recordkeeping
Employers must maintain logs of work-related injuries and illnesses and investigate incidents to identify root causes and corrective actions.
