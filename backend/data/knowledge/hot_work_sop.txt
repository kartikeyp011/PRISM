Hot Work Permit Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Document ID: sop-hot-work-001

1. Scope
This SOP applies to all welding, cutting, brazing, and other hot work activities in designated plant zones.

2. Pre-work requirements
Before starting hot work, the following must be completed:
- Obtain an active hot work permit from the permit office
- Perform atmospheric gas monitoring (LEL) continuously in the work zone
- Assign a trained fire watch for the duration of work plus 30 minutes after completion
- Isolate or shield combustible materials within 35 feet of the work area
- Post hot work warning signage at zone entry points

3. Gas monitoring thresholds
- Lower Explosive Limit (LEL) must remain below 10% before work begins
- Work must stop immediately if LEL exceeds 15%
- Fire watch must have a functional extinguisher within arm's reach

4. Permit authorization
Hot work permits are zone-specific. Workers must remain within the authorized zone polygon for the duration of the permit.

5. Emergency response
If gas levels spike or fire is detected, evacuate the zone, notify control room, and activate the site emergency response plan.
