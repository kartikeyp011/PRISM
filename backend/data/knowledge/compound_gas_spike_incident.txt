Incident Report Summary: Compound Gas Spike Near-Miss

Document ID: incident-compound-gas-001

Date: 2026-06-15
Location: Confined Space A, Demo Refinery
Status: Closed — corrective actions implemented

Summary
During scheduled maintenance, a hot work permit was active in Confined Space A while LEL sensors detected rising gas levels. A worker entered the zone before gas levels were confirmed safe. LEL peaked at 58% before work was stopped.

Contributing factors
- Hot work permit issued while gas monitoring showed elevated LEL readings
- Worker entry occurred before atmospheric verification was complete
- Compound risk condition: simultaneous hot work, gas spike, and zone occupancy

Corrective actions
- Reinforced requirement to halt hot work when LEL exceeds 15%
- Updated permit-to-work checklist to require dual sign-off on gas readings
- Deployed PRISM compound risk detection for real-time correlation of permits, sensors, and worker location

Lessons learned
Compound conditions involving permits, gas readings, and worker presence require automated correlation — single-sensor thresholds alone are insufficient.
